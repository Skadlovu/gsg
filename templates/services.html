Research and Development

agricultural research service we will be working with countries in the world’s dry areas, supporting them for the sustainable productivity of their agricultural production systems; increased income for smallholder farmers living on dry lands and in fragile ecosystems, and national food security based-solutions that include new crop varieties.  

Research Program service will bring together a wide range of partners, including countries, research and development organizations to bring rural communities living in the world’s dry areas practical solutions for improved livelihoods and food security. The goal of is to identify and develop resilient, diversified and more productive combinations of crop, livestock, rangeland, aquatic and agroforestry systems that increase productivity, reduce hunger and malnutrition, and improve quality of life among the rural poor. To develop solutions, research teams - in partnership with rural communities and countries - will validate the effectiveness of interventions in representative agro-ecosystems, and promote their scaling-out in the dry areas.

AGricultural Planing

GREEN SOIL GROUP will provide support to farmers by providing Strategies to Increase Agricultural Productivity and Reduce Land Degradation,   Land degradation and low agricultural productivity are severe problems in South African countries. Although soils were once considered to be among the most fertile in the world problems of soil nutrient depletion, erosion, and other manifestations of land degradation appear to be increasing. The rate of soil nutrient depletion is among the highest in sub-Saharan Africa, and soil erosion is a serious problem. Degradation contributes to the low and in many cases declining agricultural productivity for farmers.   Finding ways to reverse these trends is an urgent need and many other developing farmers. In order to do that, information is needed to help identify strategies that will lead to more productive and sustainable land use. We will provide our service by also helping farmers who wish to expand their cultivation growth by a number of hectors, our service will be to test the soil, site preparation, watering, planting, preparing of irrigation systems, etc

Organifood Stores

Food markets vary greatly from country to country and even from province to province, depending on the local culture, socioeconomic conditions, food varieties and dietary preferences. However, all food markets should have one major thing in common – they should provide the community with safe and nutritious food. This essential function is at the centre of the World Health Organization’s Healthy Food Markets initiative. Given the differences among markets, the implementation of Healthy Food Markets pilot projects will also differ greatly in various regions of the world.

GREEN SOUL GROUP is intended as a basic orientation and reference point for developing a Healthy Food Market pilot project. While the principles do indeed apply to all markets, some markets may require flexibility for achieving their goals. Successful Healthy Food Market pilot projects are ones where the community comes together to work for a common vision of a safe and healthy market. Regardless of whether specific improvements can be achieved in one month, one year or ten years, working towards their vision of a Healthy Food Market will ultimately mean better health and nutritional status for everyone in the community. GREEN SOIL GROP is aiming to establish its own number of chain stores that will be mainly be managed by the group, supported, and maintained, all these products that are being manufactured in the GREEN SOIL GROUP agricultural industrial will be transported in these chain stores for consumes to buy, we will also not close our doors to other supermarkets, whom will have interest in our products, out mission is to bring the healthy life style of buying good food.
